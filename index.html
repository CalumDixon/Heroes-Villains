Local farmer terrorised by a swan

Local Farmer Duncan Brindley claims Swan is ruining his life.

On the outskirts of London in Eton, local farmer Duncan Brindley has formed a peculiar rivalry with an avian foe. A local swan from a pond nearby to his farm has decided to claim one of Brindley’s plots of land as his own. Despite his best efforts Brindley has not managed to make the swan abandon the plot of land, his efforts to chase the bird away have only resulted in its return shortly after his absence. Any effort to stop the bird from returning also seem to have failed, each time the bird has managed to maneuver each obstacle, with the issue escalating to the point of the plot of land featuring a barbed wire fence, a small ditch moat surrounding the plot, and animal traps hidden throughout the plot.

***

As of today the local authorities have become involved in the matter after concerns were raised by local citizens regarding the swan potentially causing a smaller harvest due to crop damage. Constable Danny Butterman & Sergeant Nicholas Angel have been tasked with removing the bird from the property. Butterman & Angel made the statement that they have decided to relocate the swan to one of the next villages over of called Winkfield. Expect updates regarding any further advancements in the case.

Man missing for 8 years returns home

Scottish Fisherman Markus Boyd, returns home after 8 years missing

For the 4 decades, the fisheries of Norway and the British Isles have been locked in fierce competition over fishing territories within the Norwegian Sea. Scotsman Markus Boyd was one of many affected, having left for sea in unfavourable conditions due to falling behind on catch targets, Boyd was blown off course and destroyed his ship against the cliff sides of the Norwegian coast. Boyd like most fisherman wasn't taking money with him on his trips, so was forced to stay in Norway for some time due to lacking the necessary funds to pay for a ferry home. During his stay Boyd struggled to find work as a result of the language barrier, resulting in him having to slowly hitchhike from Norway to France where he managed to find English speaking fisherman willing to take him across the English channel.

Pastor of Chigwell church convicted

Pastor Arthur Hastings is found guilty of stealing funds from the church

Some 4 months ago it was uncovered by Jane Lacksnatch that Pastor Arthur Hastings of the Chigwell Evangelical Church has been stealing funds from donations made to the church, resulting in £560 worth of funds being stolen from the church. Miss Lacksnatch has reportedly stated that she discovered this fact when making an unannounced visit to the Pastor to surprise him with a Christmas gift. Yesterday morning, Judge David Livingstone charged Pastor Hastings of both misappropriation of funds and theft, with a sentence of 4 months in prison.

***

This resulted in a large outcry from the citizens of Chigwell, claiming that Pastor Hastings was being given a lenient sentence because of his background in the church. Judge Livingstone has refuted this claim and made the argument that Pastor Hastings sentence is no different from a sentence he would give to any other individual convicted of the same crime. Although Judge Livingstone’s claims have been widely disregarded by the local communities as this is not the first time that such accusations have been made against Judge Livingstone.

Professor James Moriarty murders Sherlock Holmes

Scotland yard officially confirms the death of Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes in his home on 211b Baker Street

The following are the events leading up to the death of Sherlock Holmes as told by his closest friend and business partner; Dr. John H Watson. Leading up to the events of his death, Sherlock & I were investigating Professor James Moriarty, a man whom Sherlock considered his “Intellectual Equal” and referred to him as the “Napoleon of Crime.” We had aimed to uncover his criminal organisation which had accounted for the significant portion of all the crime in London.

***

Things were going well, that was until we delivered our evidence to the Scotland Yard in order to have Moriarty and his gang arrested. Evidently, Moriarty must have had an informant within the yard as it wasn't long after that Sherlock arrived unexpectedly at my home explaining that he had just experienced three separate incidents where he had been targetted by Moriarty. Since we had already handed over the necessary evidence to incriminate Moriarty's criminal enterprise, we had decided that it was best, to leave for continental Europe and wait until things calmed down. We left for Europe the next day having to go through great effort to avoid Moriarty with Sherlock going as far as to dress like an Italian Priest.

***

Eventually, we arrived at Strassburg where we were informed that the arrests had concluded but that Moriarty himself had escaped and that likely would be seeking vengeance. Sherlock had suggested that I return home for my own safety but this was out of the question, I did not want to leave my friend in his moment of need. Instead, we would across Europe, avoiding Moriarty until we would finally arrive at Meiringen, Switzerland, where things came to an end.

Miners saved from collapsed mine

Coal Miners are saved from a collapsed section of the dodworth coal mine in Yorkshire

On the 18th of April a small section of the Dodsworth Colliery in the Barnsley district of Yorkshire collapsed trapping 5 miners inside the coal mine. Luckily the well-being of the miners had been easily established with rescue teams, thanks to small cracks within the cave-in that allowed for either side to speak to one another. Thankfully the miners only sustained minor injuries and there the mineshaft didn’t experience any flooding during the collapse, this would mean that chances of survival for the miners were high and that they had time to work with, Although with no means of getting water and food to the miners it still meant that the operation would still need to be finished as soon as possible.

***

Plans to rescue the team of miners began immediately, through the assembly of a dedicated team that would work their way around the caved in section of the mine, fearing that trying to work through the collapsed rock could potentially cause further damage to the mine shaft. Through the next few days the rescue team created a tunnel towards the trapped team, eventually reaching them on the 22nd. All the miners were reported to be of good health, allowing them to return to work the following day.

How will the nation remember Sherlock Holmes?

***

With the sudden death of the beloved cultural icon, many across the country are looking back on fondly on the works published by Doyle’s as a way to remembering the deeds and actions of the now lost Sherlock Holmes. whether or not Sherlock Holmes will be a name that goes down in history as one of the greatest detective minds in history is yet to be known but what we are certain of is that he fought for what is right for the people of london.

Rhinoceros escapes the London Zoo

The London Zoo has closed temporarily, after a Rhinoceros escapes enclosure

Photo of the escaped rhinoceros

Yesterday afternoon, in the London Zoo a rhinoceros escaped its enclosure and began to roam the park freely. The incident was the result of ZooKeeper Adam Forsythe arriving for his shift in a drunken state. While in this state Forsythe failed to correctly secure the enclosure gate which allowed the rhinoceros to burst through its enclosure. Thankfully the other keepers in attendance were quick to react, and promptly evacuated members of the public into a building for their safety while they got the situation under control. Later into the evening, the zookeepers working alongside law enforcement officers were able to herd the rhinoceros into a more secluded section of the zoo, after which they proceeded to use tranquilizers to sedate the animal before locking it temporarily in the subsection of the elephant enclosure.

***

Although the emergency is over, the zoo is planned to remain closed for the next 2 days while work is done to repair damages done to the fence and to relocate the animal back to its enclosure. As for Adam Forsythe, he has parted ways with the London Zoo and has also been charged with a case of Criminal Recklessness as punishment for his actions.

Jack the Ripper case starts to run cold

After 3 years, the murders of the canonical five are yet to be solved

3 years ago in 1888, between the months of August and November, the infamous “Jack the Ripper” stalked the streets of Whitechapel, committing five murders that would become known as the canonical five. These murders in order would include; Mary Ann Nicholas, Annie Chapman, Elizabeth Stride, Catherine Eddowes and Mary Jane Kelly. Some have even speculated that the monstrous serial killer has killed far more than just the canonical five, these speculations include Rose Mylett, Alice McKenzie and even Frances Coles who was murdered only 2 months ago.

***

As time has gone on, the outlook on the investigation to catch “Jack the Ripper” has become increasingly grim, many now doubt that they will catch the criminal, instead believing that by now he has either fled the country or sufficiently covered his tracks. Despite the grim outlook the Scotland Yard have held steadfast in their claim that they will find, capture, and bring the killer to justice, but with 3 years of little progress, faith in these words is starting to falter. The best we can hope for now is that either new evidence surfaces or advancements in technology allow us to extract new clues.

Across the nation, people of the country have been up in arms regarding the competence of the police force. Recent reporting has shown that the only crimes that authorities are capable of solving are petty crimes such as theft and harassment, while larger crimes such as the “Jack the Ripper” investigation are yet to be solved after years of waiting. People are afraid that when push comes to shove and they need protecting from those with more sinister intentions than theft or fraud they will have no one to turn to. Miracle cure kills a 5th patient

False Remidies have become an epidemic in London, causing illness outbreaks from poor treatment

Across the country people have been making complaints to their local representatives in parliament for more action to be taken in tackling the epidemic of fraudulent medical treatments being sold throughout Britain.

***

These actions were stoked by the recent death of Emily Kaldwin who died after attempting to treat her Tuberculosis using the “Miracle Cure” known as Sokolov Elixir, a treatment created by the infamous Russian physician Anton Sokolov. The treatment has been marketed as cures all treatment that is capable of curing any ailment. This has led to the treatment becoming very popular across the country, particularly with Tuberculosis patients who have no means of treatment, other than rest and moving to dry climates.

***

What causes the Sokolov Elixir lethality when treating some patients is unknown. Whether it is something within the Elixir that is dangerous or if it is just an allergic reaction to the ingredients is likely to never be discovered as Mr Sokolov has been very secretive regarding the contents of his Elixir as he refuses to comment on its ingredients and what effects they might have on the human body, though it could be argued that his silence speaks for him.

Mysterious cloaked group turn out to be bird watchers

Reported cloaked group of people now identified as the Marylebone Birdwatching Society

For the past few weeks, the local authorities of London have been receiving reports of a mysterious cloaked group being spotted across the city often hiding in fields near farmland during the middle of the night. The group would reportedly try to scare citizens away who strayed too close to the group hiding in the field, leading to reporting across the city of citizens who were concerned that illegal acts were being committed in the area.

***

A short investigation was soon launched to discover if the mystery group was a hoax or not and after discovering it was fact, police constable Noel Hamilton would go on to confront the group while the sat hiding in their field. He would go on to discover that the mysterious group was actually the Marylebone Birdwatching Society who was at the time attempting to watch nocturnal barn owls. According to the Society's leader, they had been scaring off nearby locals due to them making too much noise and subsequently scaring away the nearby owls, similarly, the reason for the cloaks was to hide from the birds that are well known for their good eyesight.

Local bakery owner accused of witchery

Sara Anderson, owner of Anderson's Bakery has been accused of performing black magic

In the small village of Dogmersfield, an uproar has been started by locals after accusations were made from Lara Grant against local bakery owner Sara Anderson accusing her of performing black magic and poisoning the people of the town. Grant bases her claims on allegedly being an eyewitness to Anderson speaking to her pet black cat whom she believes to be Anderson’s familiar.

***

Most locals ignored Grant’s calls to action against Anderson, dismissing them as absurd and ridiculous, although there is a small minority who have joined Grant in her protest. This has resulted in local authorities having to become involved in the matter after Anderson had reported that Grant and others had been watching her house at night and vandalised her property with posters telling her to leave the village. When local authorities confronted Grant regarding these claims she agreed that Anderson’s account was true but then went on to defend her actions as an act of neighbourhood protection. Shortly afterward Grant was charged with for acts of disturbing the peace and harassment, resulting in 3 months in prison.
